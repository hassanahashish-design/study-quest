FAQ

What is BrainRot?

BrainRot is a free study app that turns your notes into a Duolingo-style game. You drop in a PDF and it builds a quiz with levels, streaks and flashcards, so revising feels like playing.

How does it work?

Upload a PDF or text file of your notes. BrainRot reads the text in your browser, sends it to be turned into questions, and builds a full quest — multiple-choice questions, a why explanation for each, and flashcards — in a few seconds.

Is BrainRot free?

Yes. As of July 2026 BrainRot is free while it's in beta. A paid Pro plan with class sharing and unlimited generation is planned for later.

What files can I upload?

PDFs and plain-text files. The PDF needs real, selectable text — a scanned page or photo of notes has no text layer, so BrainRot can't read it yet.

Does it work on my phone?

Yes. BrainRot runs in any modern browser on phone, tablet or laptop. There's no app to install and no account needed to start.

How is it different from Quizlet or Kahoot?

Those give you flashcards or class quizzes. BrainRot turns your notes into a single-player Duolingo-style journey — a levelled map with streaks and a two-step answer-then-understand-why format built for real retention.
